# Voice of Local Diabetic User

## Top Frustrations

- Records live in portals, paper folders, discharge packets, texts, and emails.
- Supply refills, device backups, batteries, snacks, socks, shoes, transport, and forms become a second job.
- Family members either know too little or monitor too much.
- People are embarrassed to ask for food, rides, cleaning, foot-care help, or money for supplies.
- Post-hospital instructions are easy to lose and hard to translate into daily routines.
- Foot wounds create fear, but people need clinician-directed support, not internet advice.

## Hidden Needs

- A private way to ask for help without shame.
- A trusted person or desk to sort non-medical logistics.
- A clean emergency sheet that family can find.
- A supply reserve list that avoids panic.
- Someone to verify whether a vendor is real, insured, and safe.
- Reminder setup that works on the phone/watch people already use.

## Quote-Style User Statements

- “I do not need another app yelling at me. I need help knowing what matters today.”
- “When I got home from the hospital, I had papers, prescriptions, appointments, and fear. I needed a checklist.”
- “I want my family to know what to do, but I do not want to feel watched.”
- “I need help asking for supplies without feeling like a burden.”
- “I want local vendors I can trust around my health and my home.”

## Practical Outcomes People Want

- Emergency sheet created.
- Supplies organized.
- Reminder schedule configured.
- Local support contacts verified.
- Care pack delivered or picked up.
- Family updated with permission.
- Records organized locally.
- Next appointment prep completed.


## Non-Negotiable Doctrine

LocalDiabetic does not harvest diabetic data. It coordinates practical local help while keeping health records local-first wherever possible. It does not diagnose, prescribe, dose medication, triage emergencies, or replace licensed medical professionals. Every workflow must have explicit consent, human review, service boundaries, and a path to licensed care or emergency services when needed.
