# LocalDiabetic Executive Summary

LocalDiabetic is the real-world deployment layer for OpenDiabetic Foundation. OpenDiabetic owns and operates privacy-first diabetic compute infrastructure; LocalDiabetic turns that compute into local help: care packs, vetted vendors, reminders, family workflows, NAS/local vaults, edge helpers, resource maps, and human-in-the-loop support desks.

## Thesis

People living with diabetes do not only need glucose apps. They need help organizing the daily operating system around diabetes: records, supplies, food, foot care, transportation, reminders, family communication, post-hospital recovery, and local services.

## Recommended MVP

1. LocalDiabetic.com static landing page.
2. Palm Beach County pilot directory and partner target list.
3. Care pack request, donor, vendor, volunteer, and clinic interest forms with strict non-medical disclaimers.
4. Three MVP care packs: Starter, Foot Protection, Post-Hospital Recovery.
5. Human-reviewed care desk workflow using email/ticketing, not automated medical triage.
6. Synology NAS vault demo for local records, emergency sheet, supply inventory, and family permission patterns.
7. Apple Watch/iPhone reminder setup guide.
8. Monthly impact report: requests, packs, vendors, volunteers, satisfaction, incidents.

## Pilot Recommendation

Start with a **church/community center + podiatry/wound-care referral hybrid pilot** in Palm Beach County. This model has trust, local presence, practical needs, and manageable scope. Avoid hospital discharge integration until SOPs, consent, and liability controls are proven.

## Top 10 Insights

1. Diabetes is a 24/7 operating burden, not a single clinical metric.
2. Local help is fragmented across clinics, pharmacies, food banks, transportation, home help, and family.
3. Foot care and post-hospital recovery create urgent non-medical support needs but must stay clinician-directed.
4. Care packs are the cleanest early proof of value because they convert support into visible action.
5. Trust comes from local presence, transparent boundaries, privacy doctrine, and human review.
6. The first product should coordinate, not diagnose.
7. Vendors must be vetted with insurance/licensing/background checks where relevant.
8. NAS/local vault is a differentiator, but support burden must be carefully scoped.
9. Donations and grants fit better than data monetization.
10. LocalDiabetic should measure dignity, reduced chaos, and follow-through, not only app engagement.


## Non-Negotiable Doctrine

LocalDiabetic does not harvest diabetic data. It coordinates practical local help while keeping health records local-first wherever possible. It does not diagnose, prescribe, dose medication, triage emergencies, or replace licensed medical professionals. Every workflow must have explicit consent, human review, service boundaries, and a path to licensed care or emergency services when needed.
