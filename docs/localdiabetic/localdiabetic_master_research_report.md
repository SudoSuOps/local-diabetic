# LocalDiabetic Master Research Report

## Answer to the Strategic Question

LocalDiabetic becomes the trusted local support node for diabetic life by doing what apps and clinical systems do not do well: coordinating practical, human, local support while keeping data ownership with the person. The model is care packs + vetted vendors + family permission workflows + reminders + local vaults + human-reviewed care desk + privacy-first infrastructure from OpenDiabetic.

## Top 10 LocalDiabetic Insights

1. Local support is the missing layer between clinical care and everyday diabetic life.
2. Trust is operational, not rhetorical: vet vendors, disclose limits, answer requests, and protect data.
3. Care packs are the fastest proof of value.
4. Foot and wound support are high-value but high-risk; LocalDiabetic must stay organization-only and clinician-directed.
5. Seniors and post-hospital patients are likely early high-need users.
6. Family coordination must balance visibility with dignity.
7. NAS/local vault is a differentiator but should start as a guided demo.
8. Palm Beach County is a practical pilot geography because community, senior, and health-resource density are high.
9. Vendor trust network must include insurance/licensing/background checks by category.
10. The business model must be donations, grants, sponsorships, paid optional setup, and transparent vendor partnerships, never diabetic data monetization.

## Recommended MVP

A static LocalDiabetic.com site with non-medical request forms, care-pack pilot, vendor/volunteer intake, Palm Beach partner directory, emergency sheet templates, Apple Watch reminder guide, and Synology vault demo.

## Recommended First Pilot

Palm Beach County church/community center + podiatry/wound-care advisor hybrid. Serve 25 requests and deliver 25 packs in 90 days.

## Recommended First 7 Days

1. Build LocalDiabetic landing page.
2. Publish privacy/no-medical-advice doctrine.
3. Create request/donor/vendor/volunteer forms.
4. Finalize first 3 care packs.
5. Create vendor vetting checklist.
6. Contact 20 Palm Beach partners.
7. Recruit clinical boundary advisor and community host.


## Non-Negotiable Doctrine

LocalDiabetic does not harvest diabetic data. It coordinates practical local help while keeping health records local-first wherever possible. It does not diagnose, prescribe, dose medication, triage emergencies, or replace licensed medical professionals. Every workflow must have explicit consent, human review, service boundaries, and a path to licensed care or emergency services when needed.
